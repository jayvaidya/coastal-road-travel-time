How accurate is this?

The figure is Google's live travel-time estimate for the segment. Google produces it by combining anonymised location data from phones currently on the road with historical patterns for that time and place, together with reported incidents. Because each reading is taken live, it reflects conditions at that moment, including jams, accidents and weather slowdowns, rather than a fixed average of a typical day.

It is an estimate, not a measurement of any single vehicle, but it is the same figure shown to the public in Google Maps. Google reports that its arrival-time estimates are accurate for the large majority of trips, and independent researchers have used the same Google travel-time data to study congestion in Indian cities.

Why measure this?

A civic decision about this stretch of road is under discussion, but the conversation has so far rested on impressions and anecdote rather than measurement. People disagree about how long the exit actually takes, and how often it is slow.

This project exists to inform that decision with open data. It records the travel time continuously and publishes every reading, so that anyone can examine the evidence and reach their own conclusion. It does not argue for any particular outcome. Its only aim is to make the facts about travel time on this segment clear, public, and verifiable.
